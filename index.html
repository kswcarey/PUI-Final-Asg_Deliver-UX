Personas

Personas are a great way to synthesize your research findings after gathering detailed information about your users through observation, interviews, and surveys. Personas are archetypes of user behaviors that help generate empathy for your users within your product team.

Journey Map

Journey maps are a visual representation of the physical, mental, and emotional process your user goes through when using your product. Create one journey map of the current state and another of the desired future state. From there, you can imagine how your product would fill in the gaps.

Examples

Great step towards:

  • Generating empathy

Scenarios

Scenarios are short, written narratives of the situation, solution, and resolution with your user at the center. Once your team has digested the research, work together to imagine a variety of solutions for your user’s main poin points. Use this as an exercise to explore as many different solutions as your team can produce.

Storyboards

Storyboards give life to your solution. Set up the problem, solution, and resolution for your user through short narratives and simple drawings. Storyboards can be used with your team to better understand the user’s context. You can also show them to users to get initial feedback on solutions before creating detailed designs.

Examples

Sequence Flow Model

Sequence flow models visualize the sequence of events from the point of view of one user, based on your research of user behavior. The model outlines who your user interacts with, how long actions take, and the flow of information and artifacts. By visualizing these steps, you can find the holes that your solution could fill.

Examples

Great step towards:

  • Synthesizing user research
  • Scoping project

User Flow

User flows show the sequence of actions a user would perform with your solution. This could include displaying information, user decisions, and user inputs. User flows are a great to identify what screens should be made and what to include on those screens. They also help identify potential errors before development.

Examples

Wireframe

Wireframes are the foundation for the content of your solution. They help organize the elements of a page to prepare your design for user feedback and development. Wireframes include placeholders for content, such as text and images, and annotations to communicate to your team and developers about how interactions will work.

Examples

Great step towards:

Paper Prototypes

Paper prototypes are a type of lo-fi prototype for initial user feedback on the design of your solution. Make the interactions of your solution physical before representing them digitally through hi-fi prototypes or coding. This clarifies interactions and uncovers issues with your design while they are still easy to fix.

Examples

Great step towards:

High Fidelity Prototypes

Hi-fi prototypes are final screen designs that are clickable and represent the interactions that will be possible in the full solution. They are not fully functioning in the backend, but are still essential for inviting user feedback, showcasing to clients, and illustrating design details to developers. There are many tools available for hi-fi prototype creation for mobile and web.

Time to Iterate!

If you made it through your initial round of designs but still find yourself stuck, it’s time to get user feedback and iterate on your designs. There are always ways for you to better attend to your user’s needs. The number of iterations you do is largely dependent on your time and budget constraints, but it is an increasingly important part of the agile method.
